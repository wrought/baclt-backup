BACLT Board of Directors


The Bay Area Community Land Trust is a non-profit membership organization run by an elected Board of Directors and managed by an Executive Director.


Board President Beth Youhn has been on the board since 2006.  Now retired, she is the former Executive Director of Tradeswomen Inc., a nonprofit that supports tradeswomen, and assists unions and employers to recruit and retain skilled tradeswomen. Ms. Youhn worked as a heavy equipment operator with Operating Engineers Local 3 for 20 years, and later worked to promote construction careers for women and minorities at Laney Community College and the City and Port of Oakland.  She serves on the California Apprenticeship Council’s Blue Ribbon Committee on Women in Apprenticeship, is a founding board member of Tradeswomen Now and Tomorrow, a national policy coalition, and is a founding member of the AFL-CIO Building and Construction Trades Department’s Standing Committee on Women in the Trades.


Board Secretary Marie Whidden is a founding member of BACLT.  She has served actively as a member of the Membership Committee and assisted with many of BACLT’s events.  She has been active in the community for 40 years, sponsoring youth groups, directing improvisational theater groups and volunteering in the schools.  She has been a massage therapist and teacher for 31 years.  She is a long-time co-op supporter, was a member of the Berkeley Food Co-op, and lived in a co-op in the 1980s.

Board treasurer MJ Kroll is a singer, songwriter, real estate consultant and community activist.   MJ became a member of the BACLT while researching how to develop an artist co-operative. She has been an independent singer-songwriter for 13 years.  MJ has several years experience organizing and hosting showcases to promote local music and to support benefits and charities.  She holds a degree in real estate finance and a minor in urban studies and has worked with hundreds of small businesses over the last 20 years as a commercial property manager.  is currently working as a property manager with Madison Park Financial who specializes in multi-family, mixed-use and live/work development and management.


Jesse Palmer has been an attorney since 1995 and has represented housing coops, shared housing arrangements and non-profit corporations since 2000. He  received his JD from University of California, Hastings College of the Law and a BS from the University of California, Berkeley. He has lived in group housing situations continuously since 1987 and founded founded a seven person communal house in Berkeley in 1998 where he still lives.


Chris Bailey was elected to the board in 2009, after being a being a BACLT member for four years.  She was one of the early women members of the Carpenters Union in the 1970's and an officer in the Oakland chapter in the 1980's.   She went to Nicaragua to build houses on a coffee cooperative in 1985.   Ms. Bailey worked as a building inspector for Alameda County for many years and now is a building inspector/plans examiner for the city of San Francisco.  She is a past vice-president of her SEIU local.


Christopher Canzonieri was elected to the board in 2013. he lives at the Derby-Walker House Co-op, Berkeley and currently serves as the co-op's treasurer and by default the co-ops landscaper.  Mr. Canzonieri played an active role with BACLT during the formation of Derby Walker Co-op.  He has a MA in anthropology and is employed in the private sector where he serves as lead archaeologist, physical anthropologist and Native American liaison and facilitator for a small locally owned archaeology firm.


Stephen Barton recently retired from the City of Berkeley after serving as Housing Director, Deputy Director of the Rent Stabilization Board and as a housing planner.  He has a Ph.D. in City and Regional Planning from the University of California, Berkeley and is the author of numerous articles on housing policy and economics.  He received an Affordable Housing Leadership award from the Non-Profit Housing Association of Northern California and a National Planning Award from the American Planning Association in 1997.


Marcia Caine Edelen joined the board in 2014.  She grew up in Berkeley flatlands and her family is all still in Bay Area. She was member and a front-line employee of Consumers Co-op of Berkeley.  She has been a Board member of the Co-op Center Credit Union and a member of the West Berkeley Fund for Community Progress and the Berkeley Climate Action Coalition's community outreach task force. She has been an active volunteer at BACLT for several years.


Executive Director Rick Lewis has eighteen years of experience in housing and economic development, specializing in the development and support of housing cooperatives.  He has over twenty years experience as a carpenter and general contractor.  He was a founding member of BACLT and served on the Board from 2006 to 2010.  He has served on the Berkeley Housing Advisory Commission and on the San Francisco Community Land Trust board, and lives in a housing co-op.


Adam Maloon, Board Treasurer, was appointed to the board in 2015.   Currently, at Bay Area Legal Aid, he works with tenants in buildings at risk of losing affordability, working to provide the training, education, and organizing residents need to exert effective control over their housing. Mr. Maloon worked with the Northern California Land Trust, where he performed research and advocacy and assisted homeowners with corporate matters. During law school, he participated in the Harrison Institute for Housing and Community Development, where he represented Limited Equity Housing Cooperatives seeking financing and corporate guidance.  Mr. Maloon received his J.D. from Georgetown University Law Center and is an active member of the California State Bar.
